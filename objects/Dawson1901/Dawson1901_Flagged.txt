CHAPTER IV.

OTTER HUNTING ON THE __place__SANTA BARBARAS.

Some of Dye's shipments to the __place__islands proving unremunerative from the great waste in handling such brittle lumber, he closed out the business at __place__Santa Cruz. He decided to try otter or seal hunting, and invited me to join him. __group__Our outfit consisted of __ship__three boats, similar to whale boats, and was made by __pers__Dye himself. They were clinker built, some fifteen feet long, and carried three men each: the hunter on the front seat, with three rifles and a paddle; a hand in midship with a paddle; and a steersman behind, with a paddle and a mast and sail to use when desired. We took provisions and ammunition in the boats.

Setting out with __pers__Dye as hunter in one boat, I in another, and a man by the name of __pers__Simmons in in [sic] the third, we coasted from __pers__Monterey down to __place__Santa Barbara, and out to and around __place__Santa Rosa and __place__San Miguel Islands, killing a few otter.

Our mode of procedure was this: We traveled by sail or paddle to a place where otters were known to be found, and waited for a favorable day. Very few spots suitable for camps were found. There must be fresh water, and a place where the boats can land through the surf without capsizing. We often ventured to land at spots where it was risky. This we did by approaching and waiting for a big roller; then, by vigorous strokes&#8212;the steersman keeping her bow straight on&#8212;we would go in on top of it, and be sent so far up as to give time to unload and carry boat and all up out of the reach of succeeding billows.

The otter were found lying on the kelp with only the head in sight, and always in bunches, or schools. They are shot in the head, and can best be hunted when the water is still. Approaching noiselessly and placing our boats so that the otter were in the center, we would begin the battle, loading and firing rapidly until the otter were all scattered. Then, picking up our game, we would give chase to one of the runaways. Here the difficulty was in keeping track of the game, as they travel under the water and only come to the surface for breath. The hunter has seen the direction of the dive and paddles in that direction, ready to fire when the otter's head comes above the surface. The chase is kept up until the otter is killed or the trail lost; then if no other otter can be started, the hunt is up; and as soon as skinning, fleecing, and drying is through with, a travel is made to another locality.

Otter hunting is a very exciting business, and somewhat dangerous, as you are apt to think when you hear a bullet whistle past you, it having glanced from a wave as it came from a hunter's gun opposite, who had shot at an otter on a line between you and him. But these risks will be run for an otter skin, worth thirty dollars, and especially when there is rivalry among the hunters.

At __place__Santa Barbara we found an old otter hunter, __pers__A. B. Thompson, who had formerly been in the business with vessels and __group__Alaska Indians, and who proposed to go into partnership with us on the halves&#8212;he to furnish a vessel officered and manned, and to provision us; we to furnish boats, guns and ammunition and do the hunting. His offer was accepted; and __group__two Santa Barbara hunters, __pers__Sparks and __pers__Nydever, joined in with us, making, with __pers__Dye, __pers__Simmons and myself, __ship__five boats. The advantage in having a vessel was that with water and provisions on board the __group__hunters could be lowered at any point, the __ship__vessel cruising around until we got through, and in this way places inaccessible for boats could be hunted.

We first visited __place__San Nicholas Island, and, hunting around it, killed several otter. Here we searched on shore for a __pers__lone woman whom we knew to be on the island, but failed to find her, although we found her tracks. Why she was there I had best explain:

Such of the __place__Santa Barbara Islands as had fresh water on them had once been inhabited by a low type of __org__Indians, who eked out a miserable existence by fishing for and eating shellfish. Some were induced to emigrate and join the __org__missions, but a remnant remained, and these being about to perish from starvation, an effort was made, headed by the __group__priests, to have them all transported to the __place__mainland. A __ship__vessel was sent, and at __place__San Nicholas, after all were on board, this __pers__woman, from some freak, jumped overboard and swam ashore. (Since that time I have seen an account of her rescue by __pers__George Nydever and __pers__Charley Brown, both of whom I knew. They found her while they were on an otter hunt, and carried her to the __place__mainland, where she soon died from overeating.)

On the brig __ship__Bolivar, we hunted all points along __place__Lower California to __palce__Ceros Island, then, otter giving out, we abandoned the hunt and beat back against headwinds to __place__Santa Barbara. Here a general division and setlting up was effected, and I found myself with forty dollars in money and five or six otter skins, a broken arm, and my feet so badly bruised that I could scarcely walk on crutches.

My physical condition happened in this way: __ship__The vessel had dropped us on the island of __place__Ceros to hunt, if favorable weather intervened, while she made further search below for otter. Soon after we left __place__camp I, in order to get a shot at some spotted seals, ventured too near the brink of a projecting point. My feet flew up, I could find nothing to grasp, and I went over. But I yelled, gave my gun a cast that it might fall flat, and straightened my legs under me to lessen the shock. I do not know how far I fell, but when I came to, my __group__comrades were around me, I was on a bed of sand, and all around were big rocks detached from the cliff above. On examination it was found that my left arm was broken, and my feet and legs so bruised that I could not walk. One man went back to __place__camp for a sail, from which a litter was made, and I was carried to __place__camp. __pers__Dye attempted to set my arm, but it remains till now a little crooked, and weaker than the other. My feet and ankles, though bloodshot for awhile [sic], in time became as sound as ever.
